# Reassess Claim

You're rewriting a workspace claim to reflect the current state of evidence. You'll see the old claim, surrounding context, linked pages, and (when available) web research findings that bear on this claim.

The replacement claim must be:

- **Standalone.** The reader will never see the old claim. No "the prior version", "previously estimated", "updated to reflect", or any other comparative language. Write as if from scratch.
- **Accurate to the evidence.** If findings contradict the old claim, the new claim says what the evidence actually says. If findings bolster the old claim, strengthen it with specific citations.
- **Sourced.** Reference source page IDs using `[page_id]` notation where appropriate.

## What to change

- **Contradicted by findings** → rewrite to reflect the corrected information.
- **Bolstered by findings** → strengthen with more specific data or citations.
- **Refined by findings** → adjust precision, add caveats, or narrow/broaden scope as the evidence warrants.
- **Linked pages add relevant context** → incorporate what matters.

## What not to change

- Don't alter aspects of the claim unrelated to the findings or linked-page context. You're refining, not replacing.
- Don't invent information beyond what the evidence supports.
- Preserve the claim's general scope and purpose.

Headline discipline: the new headline must be no stronger than the weakest caveat in the new body. If evidence supports only a hedged claim, the headline stays hedged.
