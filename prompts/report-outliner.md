You are preparing an outline for a research report that will distill the findings of an extensive investigation.

You will be given:

* The top-level research question
* The main subquestions that were investigated
* The judgements reached on each subquestion and on the top-level question
* A map of the research tree showing how subquestions relate to each other and to the top-level question

Your task is to produce an outline for a report that communicates the most important findings to a human reader. Your output will be structured according to the provided JSON schema.

The research process has been pursued earnestly, but may contain errors of judgement. Your goal is to help the reader understand the answer to the question as well as possible -- not just to report the outputs of the research process. Think independently about how best to present the material.



Guidelines for the outline:

* Prioritize ruthlessly. The investigation may have explored many branches. Not all of them deserve space in the report. Focus on findings that are most important, most surprising, or most decision-relevant. It is better to cover five things well than fifteen things superficially.
* Lead with conclusions. The reader wants to know what you found, then why you believe it. Structure sections so that the key takeaway comes first, followed by the reasoning and evidence.
* Make the narrative arc clear. The sections should flow logically. The reader should understand why each section follows the previous one. If the investigation revealed that the answer depends critically on some key variable or subquestion, structure the report around that.
* Flag uncertainty honestly. If the investigation reached confident conclusions on some subquestions but not others, the outline should reflect this. Don't bury uncertainty. A section on "key uncertainties and open questions" is often valuable.
* Think about what's actionable. If the findings have implications for decisions or further investigation, the outline should include space for this, likely toward the end.
* Prioritize considerations over conclusions. The most important thing the report can convey is the considerations bearing on the question, so that the reader can form their own view. Your judgements matter too -- they help the reader orient and know which considerations deserve the most attention -- but they should serve the considerations, not replace them.
* Be specific in notes\_for\_drafter. The instance drafting each section will see the outline and the pages you specify. If there's something important about how to handle the material -- a tension to address, a caveat to include, a framing to use or avoid -- say so in notes\_for\_drafter.
