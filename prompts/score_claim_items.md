# Claim Investigation Item Scoring

You are evaluating items produced during a claim investigation. These may be claims (how-true stories, how-false stories, cruxes) or questions (evidence questions, stress-test cases, crux-questions). Your job is to score each item on two dimensions:

- **Impact** (0-10): How much would resolving or understanding this item shift the overall assessment of the scope claim? A score of 10 means it is a critical crux whose resolution would substantially change credence. A score of 0 means it is tangential or redundant.

- **Fruit** (0-10): How much useful investigation can the system still apply to this item? A score of 10 means the item is wide open with many unexplored angles. A score of 0 means it has been thoroughly investigated or is unanswerable with available tools.

For each item, provide brief reasoning (1-2 sentences) explaining your scores. Focus on the marginal value of further investigation given what has already been discovered.
