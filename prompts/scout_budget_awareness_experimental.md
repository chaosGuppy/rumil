# Prioritiser Budget Awareness

The prioritiser that will act on your output has a **total remaining budget of {budget} units** to spend on further investigation of the scope question. Shape what you surface accordingly.

## Why this matters

Every question, hypothesis, estimate, analogy, paradigm case, or factcheck you surface becomes a candidate for the prioritiser to invest in. A question that only pays off after deep, sustained investigation is worse than useless if the prioritiser cannot afford that depth — the prioritiser will either skip it (your work is wasted) or fund it partially (it pays for the setup and leaves before the payoff).

The quality of your output depends on how well its impact-effort profile matches the budget the prioritiser actually has.

## Effort tiers

Think of the items you surface as falling into rough effort tiers, based on how much investigation they would need before they meaningfully move the answer to the scope question:

- **Low effort** — a handful of considerations, a single web lookup, or a quick sharpening pass is enough to get most of the value.
- **Medium effort** — needs a focused mini-investigation: a few rounds of scouting and sharpening, maybe one or two web lookups.
- **High effort** — only pays off after sustained investigation: multiple rounds of scouting, sub-investigations of its own, and significant sharpening.

An item's effort tier is a property of its **shape**, not of how interesting it is. A genuinely important question that cannot be usefully addressed without a high-effort investigation is still high-effort — and surfacing it when the budget cannot support that effort is worse than surfacing nothing, because it crowds out items the prioritiser can actually act on.

## What to prefer at each budget level

- **Budget ≤ 5** — Low-effort only. Surface items where a cheap follow-up pass meaningfully moves the answer. Skip anything that needs a real investigation — it will either sit unfunded or be funded into a half-measure.
- **Budget 5-15** — Mostly low-effort. At most one medium-effort item, and only if its shape is genuinely promising. Skip high-effort items.
- **Budget 15-40** — Low- and medium-effort items are both in scope. One high-impact medium-effort item can earn the majority of the budget if its shape warrants it. Still avoid high-effort items unless they are exceptional.
- **Budget 40-80** — High-effort items become first-class. A small number can earn substantial investigation; low- and medium-effort items fill out the rest. Slow-burn, threshold-shaped, and unbounded lines of inquiry can all be appropriate here.
- **Budget 80+** — Almost no restriction. Even items that only pay off after sustained investigation are worth surfacing; the prioritiser has the budget to sustain them.

## Rule of thumb

For each item you are about to surface, ask: *what tier of effort would this need before it meaningfully moves the answer to the scope question?* If the answer is higher than what the current budget of {budget} can support (per the bands above), do not surface it. Save the slot for something whose shape actually fits the budget.

Err on the side of immediately-impactful-but-modestly-valuable items over high-ceiling-but-deep-investment items when the budget is under 15. Reverse that priority only when the budget is genuinely large.
