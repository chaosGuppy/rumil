right now, your task is to generate new subquestions for the parent question.

first, it's worth understanding the purpose of subquestions. one of the key aims of the system within which you're operating is _context management_. the output generated while grappling with difficult, multifaceted questions can soon become overwhelming, reaching a size and a level of messiness that makes it intractible to reason over.

however, it is often possible to identify narrow research elements within a broader program that, at least to a first approximation, are _encapsulated_ or _self-contained_, in the sense that most or all of the detail involved in generating a set of findings can be hidden from a researcher working on the broader program, without any loss to that researcher. as an intuition pump, consider a mathematician who derives a lemma while working on a proof. once the lemma is derived, it can be used without reference to its derivation.

this leads us to our first criteria for a good subquestion. in order for a subquestion to be good, it must _encapsulate_ a meaningful amount of work - the more the better, all else equal. a good subquestion has a high ratio of tokens required to generate an answer to tokens required to represent an answer. a trivial lemma can simply be rederived each time, but holding onto a complex one gives real value. think also of ousterhout's "deep modules".

the second criteria is that a good subquestion has to move the investigation forward meaningfully; the more tangible progress, the better. you're aiming for a lemma that unlocks the proof, or a module that provides the system with critical functionality.

third, a good subquestion has to provide high counterfactual value relative to the set of questions that are already under investigation, assuming that we are going to generate good answers to those questions. this captures our intuition that restating the scope question (which would satisfy the first two criteria) is not valuable. it also says that, if a proposed subquestion's impact is mediated mostly by its impact on an existing subquestion, that subquestion is not valuable for the scope question (it may be valuable for the mediating subquestion, but our task here is solely to generate subquestions for the scope question). generating such a subquestion would be like asking someone to research a subcomponent of a lemma, when they're already working on the entire lemma. this final point is not derivable from what you know; please take our word that the system is designed in such a way that it is true.

now, generate up to ten good subquestions. ten is a large number here, a cap to prevent explosions, not a target to aim for. if you can think of only a single good subquestion, generate only that subquestion. if you can't think of any, that's ok; don't generate any in that case.
