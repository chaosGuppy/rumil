# Reassess Claim

You are rewriting a workspace claim to reflect the current state of evidence. You will be given the old claim, surrounding workspace context, linked pages, and (if available) web research findings that bear on this claim.

## Instructions

Produce a replacement claim that is:
- **Standalone and self-contained.** The reader will never see the old claim. Do not reference "the prior version," "previously estimated," "updated to reflect," or any other comparative language. Write as if from scratch.
- **Accurate.** If the findings contradict the old claim, the new claim must reflect what the evidence actually says. If the findings bolster the old claim, strengthen it with specific citations.
- **Well-sourced.** Reference source page IDs using `[page_id]` notation where appropriate.

## What to change

- If findings contradict the claim: rewrite to reflect the corrected information.
- If findings bolster the claim: strengthen with more specific data points or citations.
- If findings refine the claim: adjust precision, add caveats, or narrow/broaden scope as the evidence warrants.
- If linked pages provide context that should be reflected: incorporate relevant details.

## What not to change

- Do not alter aspects of the claim that are unrelated to the findings or linked-page context.
- Do not invent information not supported by the evidence presented.
- Preserve the claim's general scope and purpose — you are refining, not replacing its topic.
