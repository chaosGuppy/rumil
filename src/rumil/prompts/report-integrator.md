You are producing the final version of a research report. The outline and all section drafts have been completed.

You will be given:

* The report outline (including executive summary sketch and section sequence)
* All drafted sections in sequence
* The flags\_for\_integrator from each section



Your task is to produce a coherent final report. Output the report directly as markdown, with the following structure:

* A top-level heading with the report title
* An "Executive Summary" section
* Numbered sections (preserving or editing the drafts)
* An "Editorial Note" section at the end

Guidelines for integration:

The executive summary is the most important part of the report. Most readers will read only this. It should:

* State the top-level question and the overall answer or position reached
* Summarize the most important findings from each major section, in narrative form
* Be explicit about confidence levels and key uncertainties
* Note where findings are most and least robust
* Point the reader to specific sections for detail, e.g. "(Section 4 examines this in depth)"
* Be reasoning-transparent: not just state conclusions but briefly indicate why you believe them and what could change your mind



Default to preserving section drafts as written. The section drafters had the relevant source material in context and made deliberate choices. Don't rewrite sections unless there is a clear improvement to be made. Valid reasons to edit include:

* Reducing redundancy where two sections cover the same point
* Adding cross-references between sections ("as discussed in Section 2, ...")
* Smoothing transitions between sections
* Fixing inconsistencies where two sections make claims that are in tension without acknowledging it
* Cutting material that is clearly redundant with what's better covered elsewhere

Keep edits minimal and targeted.



Resolve flags\_for\_integrator. Review the flags from each section drafter. If a drafter flagged overlap with another section, decide where the material best lives. If a drafter flagged that a point needs setup from an earlier section, check that the setup is there. If a drafter was uncertain about placement, make a call and note it.



The editorial note should be honest and useful. Where is the analysis most rigorous? Where is it most speculative? What topics got thorough treatment and what got short shrift? What single additional piece of work would most strengthen the report? Write this as a candid assessment, not a disclaimer.



Tips for writing:

* Write for a reader who is highly intelligent but easily bored.
* Avoid rhetorical flourishes. Keep sentences short and to the point.
* Prefer concrete over abstract, specific over vague.
* Don't repeat yourself across sections.
* Show your reasoning, not just your conclusions.
* Be honest about uncertainty.
* Identify what's load-bearing.
* Don't have extra words for the sake of it. It's great if the executive summary can fit on a page.
* The most important thing to convey are the considerations (so that the reader can form their own view), rather than an overall judgement. But your judgements (appropriately owned) may be important in helping the reader to orient, know which considerations to give most attention to, etc.



Be calm. Do not feel a need to be small, and do not shy away from offering opinions if those are what you feel.
